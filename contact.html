We Accept Donations Of

  • Victorian and Edwardian prints and negatives in any condition
  • Daguerreotypes, ambrotypes, and tintypes
  • Glass plate negatives and lantern slides
  • Cartes de visite and cabinet cards
  • Albums and collections of mixed Victorian photography

A Note on Photographs If you are uncertain whether your photographs fall within our remit, please do not hesitate to enquire. We are happy to advise on photographic processes, approximate dating, and conservation needs — even if the photograph is not ultimately suitable for the archive.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

How much does a restoration cost?

Costs depend significantly on the size and condition of the original and the extent of restoration required. We provide a detailed written estimate after initial assessment, with no obligation. As a general guide, simple restorations begin at £150; complex work with extensive damage may be considerably more.

Do I need to send the original photograph?

For the highest quality results, scanning the original is strongly preferred. We can, however, work from high-resolution scans you provide, though this limits what is achievable. We handle all originals with archival care and return them promptly.

How long does restoration take?

Current turnaround time from receipt of the original to delivery of the completed digital restoration is eight to twelve weeks. Physical conservation work on the original print takes additional time. We are transparent about timelines at the point of commission.

What do I receive at the end?

You receive the original photograph returned safely, a high-resolution TIFF of the unaltered scan, a high-resolution TIFF of the restoration, a printed summary of the restoration record, and any historical research conducted. Files are supplied on archival USB or via secure download.

Will my photograph be added to the archive?

Only with your express permission. We sometimes request permission to include particularly significant photographs in the archive — but this is entirely at your discretion, and has no bearing on the restoration work or its cost.

Can you help identify people in photographs?

We cannot promise identification, but we can often provide useful historical context — the approximate date from the photographic process or costume, the likely studio if a backstamp is present, and relevant census or directory research. Many families have found this work enormously valuable.
