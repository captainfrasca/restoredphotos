The Collection

Photographic Gallery

Over two hundred restored photographs spanning the full arc of the Victorian and Edwardian ages — from the first daguerreotypes to the eve of the Great War.

✦   ✦   ✦
Restored

Robert Cornelius, Self-Portrait

Daguerreotype, 1839. The earliest known American portrait photograph.

Brig upon the Water

Gustave Le Gray, c.1856. A landmark in photographic art.

Restored

Iago — Julia Margaret Cameron

Albumen print, c.1867. Cameron's bold close-up technique was revolutionary for its time.

Regent Street, London

c.1896. Gelatin silver print. Horse-drawn omnibuses crowd the thoroughfare.

Restored

Power House Mechanic

Lewis Hine, c.1920. Hine's documentary work captured the human face of industrial labour.

Mirror Lake, Yosemite Valley

Carleton Watkins, 1865. Watkins's photographs were instrumental in the creation of Yosemite National Park.

Restored

The Photographer at Work

Studio portrait of a professional photographer with large-format camera, c.1890.

Arrival at Ellis Island

New York, c.1900. Masses of immigrants await processing at the gateway to the New World.

Restored

Animal Locomotion — Muybridge

Eadweard Muybridge, 1887. Plate 715 from the landmark sequential motion study series.

Nadar — Self-Portrait

Gaspard-Félix Tournachon, c.1865. The great Parisian portraitist photographed the luminaries of his age.

Restored

Old Market Square, Nottingham

c.1900. Market day crowds gather beneath gas lamps and Victorian shopfronts.

Boston from the Air

James Wallace Black, 1860. Taken from a balloon at 1,200 feet — the oldest surviving American aerial photograph.
